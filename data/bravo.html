Hunts & Rounds Awards

Large Hunt of the Year - MiaoHunt 2023

  • Link
  • Introduction of nominated entries from MiaoHunt 2023 (by MiaoMiaoMiao, in Chinese): Link

MiaoHunt 2023 is a large hunt organized by MiaoMiaoMiao. It is overall a great puzzle hunt, and the best part is its structure. All feeder puzzles form a complete and complex structure which is gradually revealed through multiple meta puzzles.

See Round of the Year - Backstage (Miao23) and Best Meta Puzzle - 梦幻岛的真正秘密 (True Secret of Neverland) (Miao23) for more details.

New Hunt of the Year - Fishunt 2024

  • Link

Fishunt is a hunt designed mainly by 鱼左 (FishLeft). This hunt is themed around art and many puzzles involve non-trivial art knowledge. I have seen discussions (in both Chinese- and English-speaking communities) about certain fields are underrepresented in puzzle hunts and this is a good example of an arty hunt.

Words from 鱼左 (FishLeft) Fishunt is a lightweight puzzle hunt featuring art and design. The hunt will wow you with the elaborate illustrations at your first glance, and wow you again with the ingenious ideas as well as the various art forms contained inside, at your eureka moment. Moreover, you will be amused by the humorous language style which makes your journey full with joy and laughter. I'd like to recommend you try Fishunt now!

Round of the Year - Backstage (Miao23)

  • Link

This rounds consists of three smaller rounds. Each puzzle has three different (but closely related) versions, used in each of the small rounds. Each puzzle has a unique extraction method, which is used repeatedly in the small metas. Then the meta meta gives hint of deeper structure used in the final meta.

Continued in Best Meta Puzzle - 梦幻岛的真正秘密 (True Secret of Neverland) (Miao23).

Round of the Year - Day 1 (P&KU3)

  • Link

The entire round is in the form of manga. Every puzzle (except for one) is a one-page manga. Most puzzles (including the meta) use in depth the manga format in the solving path.

Round of the Year - Day 3 (P&KU3)

  • Link

Every feeder puzzle in this round uses aspects of the protagonist (e.g., the favorite poem of the protagonist) in the solve path. Players need to figure out the correct answer to the aspects to solve the puzzle. However, the protagonist has three different versions, leading to three different answers for each of the aspects. Using different answers to each aspect, the feeder puzzles could have multiple answers (different or same). There are three meta puzzles, corresponding to three different versions of the protagonist.

Best Direction - P&KU3

  • Link
  • To clarify, this is Part 1 of P&KU3. Part 2 and 3 of P&KU3 are planned for 2025 and 2026 respectively.

Since CCBC11, Chinese puzzle hunts tend to put much emphasis on the overall structure and the final meta(s). This is great but increases construction difficulty and precludes certain kinds of novelties. P&KU3 (Part 1) explores a new direction by emphasizing special round structures and downplaying the global structure (similar to most MIT Mystery Hunts). It even does not have a final meta because this is only part 1 of a planned three-part hunt. The puzzles overall have high quality. Furthermore, the designer (Winfrid) puts a lot of effort (and money) on character design, art and story.

See also Round of the Year - Day 1 (P&KU3), Round of the Year - Day 3 (P&KU3), Best Meta Puzzle - 乞求春风再临 (Pray for the spring breeze to come again) (P&KU3).

Puzzle Awards

Best Meta Puzzle - 乞求春风再临 (Pray for the spring breeze to come again) (P&KU3)

  • Link

Feeder answers in this round have a great variety, including Chinese, English, and a date. In this meta puzzle, one needs to associate each feeder answer with a phrase in Chinese, which contains numbers in the characters. Then these numbers are put into an infinite recursive logic puzzle (fillomino), and the remaining parts of the Chinese characters are used for extraction. The solution to the logic puzzle has exactly one infinite region, and by combining Chinese character parts in the region one gets the final answer, which is an infinite periodic string "……梦中的梦中的梦中的梦……" (literal translation: "... dream in a dream in a dream in a ...").

Best Meta Puzzle - 梦幻岛的真正秘密 (True Secret of Neverland) (Miao23)

  • Link

Please read Round of the Year - Backstage (Miao23) before reading this part.

This is the final meta of MiaoHunt 2023. One uses the extracted letters in the Backstage round to construct a calendar, with each day corresponding to a letter. Furthermore, puzzles in Round 1 and 2 are matched which leads to a list of dates. Indexing these dates into the calendar leads to the final answer.

Best Logic Puzzle - 混乱星空 (Chaotic Galaxies) (Miao23)

  • Link

This is a logic puzzle. It is presented as a pixel picture and the first step is to decompose into RGB to find three spiral galaxies puzzles. However, some clues in the puzzles have the incorrect color, and need to be put into another puzzle to allow a unique solution. The logic part is difficult but has a logical solve path. The solution (found in "解析" tab) provides step-by-step solution to the full logic puzzle.

Best Interactive Puzzle - 西提沃克刮刮乐 (Citywalk Scratchcard) (CCBC15)

  • Link

This puzzle is an interactive puzzle. It requires location access of the device of the player, so the main action the solver needs to take is to walk around with a device with the puzzle page open. When the player is close to certain types of terrains, entries on the scratchcard will reveal. The player can solve the puzzle when enough information has been collected.

Best Non-Categorized Puzzle - 平行时空 (Parallel Universe) (Miao23)

  • Link

This is a conlang puzzle. It is well-constructed, uses about 50 characters and is not just a variation of a known language. It makes use of MiaoHunt Stickers (published prior to the hunt) and eventually leads to player to the pre-hunt PV, a mini-hunt by itself.

Best Introductory Puzzle - 出发, 去冒险！ (Embark on an Adventure!) (Miao23)

  • Link

This puzzle is the first puzzle in MiaoHunt 2023 and uses a few simple small puzzles to introduce common knowledge and methods often used in puzzle hunts.

Funniest Puzzle - 对不起！ (Sorry!) (CCBC15)

  • Link

This puzzle is an interactive puzzle. It uses the answer submission box as the interaction method, in a way similar to this GPH puzzle. Players need to input different answers to each question to unlock different stories and clues. The questions in each step show sense of humor. For example, the puzzle begins with (English Translation) "We did not have enough time to finish writing all puzzles. We are sorry! Could you forgive us?".

Contribution Awards

Best Non-Puzzle Contribution to the Community - 中国谜题索引 (Chinese puzzle index) (雪儿, kinami)

  • Link

This is the DevJoe Index for Chinese puzzle hunts. 雪儿 wrote the first version including the web interface and kinami rewrote the system to make it more efficient, and to allow a workflow where everyone can contribute by editing a spreadsheet, and the changes are automatically pushed to the website when a moderator approves. It currently maintains index of about 10 major Chinese hunts in the past few years.

Lifetime Achievement Award - SEVENKING

SEVENKING has made substantial contributions to the Chinese puzzle hunt community in the past 15+ years. His contributions include the following:

  • SEVENKING created the first CCBC (the largest and earliest puzzle hunt series in China) in 2008. He is the main organizer of the CCBC series and does most if not all sponsorship, art, and PR. SEVENKING is the owner of 密码菌 (Mimajun), the organization that hosts every year's CCBC.
  • SEVENKING is main moderator of Baidu Cipher Tieba since 2008. Baidu Cipher Tieba used to be the largest Chinese community for discussing classical ciphers and hunt-style puzzles. The first two CCBCs were hosted on this platform, and before 2016 (CCBC9), most discussions of CCBC and puzzle hunts in China happened here.
  • SEVENKING is the main moderator of the CCBC QQ Group, currently the second-largest Chinese community that discusses puzzle hunts, with about 2000 members.
  • SEVENKING is the maintainer of Bilibili account of 密码菌 and Wechat official account of 密码菌.

I think it is fair to say that the Chinese puzzle hunt community would not exist without his efforts.
